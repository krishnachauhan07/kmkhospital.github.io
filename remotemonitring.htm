Continuous Vital Sign Monitors

These devices track a patient's vital signs, including heart rate, blood pressure, oxygen saturation, and temperature, in real-time. They send alerts when values fall outside the predefined range, enabling rapid intervention.

Telemetry Systems

Telemetry units enable continuous monitoring of cardiac patients. Electrodes are attached to the patient, and the device transmits ECG data wirelessly to a central monitoring station, allowing healthcare professionals to monitor heart rhythms remotely.

Pulse Oximeters

These small, portable devices measure a patient's oxygen saturation levels and pulse rate. They are commonly used for monitoring respiratory conditions, especially in cases of COVID-19.

Smart Infusion Pumps

These devices control the administration of intravenous medications and fluids. They can be remotely monitored to ensure accurate dosing and to detect any anomalies.

Smart Beds

These beds are equipped with sensors to monitor patient movement, pressure points, and weight distribution. They help prevent bedsores and can alert nurses if a patient needs repositioning.

Telemedicine Carts

Equipped with cameras, microphones, and monitors, telemedicine carts facilitate remote consultations between patients and healthcare providers. They are particularly useful for virtual check-ups and specialist consultations.

Remote Patient Monitoring Apps

Patients can use smartphone apps to monitor their health data, such as blood glucose levels, and transmit the information to healthcare providers. These apps often integrate with wearable devices like glucose meters or fitness trackers.
